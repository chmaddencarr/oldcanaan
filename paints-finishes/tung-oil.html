Paints & Finishes Entry 013

Tung Oil

The penetrating finish for interior wood, and what it actually is.

Pure tung oil finish, wide plank pine floor — Connecticut.

Tung oil is one of the most misrepresented finishes in the hardware store. Almost every product sold as "tung oil" or "tung oil finish" contains little or no actual tung oil. They are varnish-based finishes with tung oil listed as an ingredient. Pure tung oil is a different material with different properties, different application, and a different result — one that is correct for traditional interior wood in a New England context.

The Character

Pure tung oil produces a low-sheen, warm, penetrating finish that allows the wood grain and color to read clearly. It does not build a film on the surface. The wood feels like wood — not like it has been coated. The sheen is similar to an oiled surface: present but not glossy. It darkens the wood slightly and enriches the grain.

What It Is

Pure tung oil is the pressed oil of the seed of the tung tree (Vernicia fordii), native to China and widely cultivated. It is a drying oil — it polymerizes when exposed to air, producing a hardened, water-resistant film within the wood pores. Applied in multiple thin coats with complete drying between coats, it builds a durable, renewable finish. Pure tung oil is available from Tried and True (Trumansburg, NY), Real Milk Paint Company, and specialty wood finishing suppliers.

How It Behaves

Pure tung oil penetrates and hardens within the wood. It is water-resistant but not waterproof — a wet glass left on a tung-oiled surface will eventually leave a mark. It is renewable: a worn area can be cleaned, lightly scuffed, and re-oiled without stripping the entire surface. This renewability is its great advantage over film-building finishes.

Why It's Right

Tung oil is the correct finish for wide plank pine floors, interior furniture-grade millwork, and wood surfaces in traditional New England interiors where the penetrating, grain-revealing character of an oil finish is the specification. It is not the correct finish where a hard, waterproof film is required.

How To Get It

Tried and True Original Wood Finish (pure tung oil polymerized without solvents) or Real Milk Paint Pure Tung Oil from specialty dealers. Do not substitute a product labeled "tung oil finish" — it is varnish, not tung oil.

The Old Canaan Standard

Pure tung oil, three to four coats applied thin and fully cured between coats, for interior wood surfaces in traditional New England buildings where a penetrating, low-sheen, grain-revealing finish is the specification. Tried and True or equivalent. This is a maintenance finish — renew every three to five years on high-wear surfaces with a single thin coat.

Common Questions

What is the difference between pure tung oil and tung oil finish?

Pure tung oil is pressed from tung tree seeds and polymerizes to a durable penetrating finish. Tung oil finish is a varnish product that may contain a small amount of tung oil but is primarily alkyd resin — it builds a film rather than penetrating. They are not the same material and do not produce the same result.

How many coats of tung oil does wood need?

Three to four coats minimum, with complete drying between coats — 24 to 48 hours per coat depending on temperature. The first coat is the most absorbed; subsequent coats build the finish. The wood is ready for use when the last coat is fully hard, typically five to seven days after the final application.

Can tung oil be used on exterior wood?

Pure tung oil is not durable enough for exposed exterior applications. It provides insufficient UV resistance and wears quickly on exterior surfaces subject to sun and rain. For exterior penetrating oil finishes, specify a purpose-formulated exterior oil. Tung oil is a correct interior finish only.
